## Tone & Voice

**Do:**
- Confident, sharp, sometimes informal, always intentional
- Sentence fragments and clean bullets over long compound phrases
- Show impact, not just action - what changed matters more than what was done
- Hook early - don't save the best metrics for the second line
- Lead with outcome, then the how

**Don't:**
- Passive or overly corporate tone
- Em-dashes (stylistic preference)
- Generic labels before getting to substance

## Anti-Patterns (Recurring Edits)

These phrases get flagged and replaced:

| Avoid | Replace With |
|-------|--------------|
| "thrive in fast-paced environments" | "my natural energy fits best in fast-moving teams" |
| "proven leader" | [specific evidence of leadership] |
| "results-driven" | [the actual results] |
| "experienced in X" | [what you did with X] |
| "strategic thinker" | [the strategy and its outcome] |
| "visionary" | [the vision and what it produced] |
| "cross-functional" (alone) | [who you worked across and what shipped] |
| Starting with "I led..." | Start with impact, then attribution |

## Resume Formatting Preferences

**Career Highlights format**: `Title: Summary` with each bullet tightly scoped to results

**Bullet structure**: Impact first, then method
- Good: "Increased ARR 47% by redesigning pricing model"
- Bad: "Redesigned pricing model, which increased ARR by 47%"

**Metrics**: Only if tied to business outcomes, not vanity metrics

**AI/ML mentions**: Only when directly relevant or substantiated - reject "AI-washing"

**0→1 framing**: Highlight work starting or scaling products, especially where you set vision and GTM
